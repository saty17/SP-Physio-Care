FAQ

Frequently Asked Question

We understand you may have questions about physiotherapy and how it can help you. Below, we’ve answered some of the most common questions our patients ask to help you feel confident about starting your recovery journey with SP Physio Care.

The right time to start physiotherapy depends on the type and severity of your injury. For minor injuries, such as sprains, you can often begin within a few days to reduce swelling and improve mobility. For post-surgical cases or fractures, it’s best to consult your doctor, but physiotherapy typically starts within 1-2 weeks to aid recovery. Always seek professional advice for a personalized timeline.

Yes, we specialize in post-fracture rehabilitation. Our physiotherapists work closely with you to restore strength, mobility, and function after a fracture. We create tailored programs that include exercises, manual therapy, and pain management techniques to ensure a safe and effective recovery.

At SP Physio Care, we prioritize personalized care. Our experienced therapists take the time to understand your unique needs and goals, creating customized treatment plans. We combine evidence-based techniques, state-of-the-art equipment, and a compassionate approach to help you recover faster and prevent future injuries.

The frequency of sessions depends on your condition and recovery goals. For acute injuries, you might need 2-3 sessions per week initially. For chronic conditions or maintenance, 1 session per week or biweekly may suffice. Your therapist will assess your progress and adjust the schedule accordingly.

Absolutely. Physiotherapy is highly effective for managing chronic pain conditions like arthritis, lower back pain, or fibromyalgia. We use techniques such as manual therapy, targeted exercises, and posture correction to reduce pain, improve movement, and enhance your quality of life.

During your first session at SP Physio Care, our therapist will conduct a thorough assessment of your condition. This includes discussing your medical history, evaluating your movement, strength, and pain levels, and setting recovery goals. We’ll then design a personalized treatment plan and may start with initial exercises or manual therapy to begin your healing process.
